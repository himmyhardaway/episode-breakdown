# Principles

These are the rules this skill follows. Every output is held to them.

---

**1. No AI slop.**
Every output must sound like it was written by a human who actually watched the episode. No hollow summaries. No generic chapter titles like "Introduction" or "Key Takeaways." No filler phrases. If it could have been generated without reading the transcript, it's wrong.

**2. Context first, always.**
The skill learns the show before it produces anything. Identity, tone, audience, and voice all come from the source — the YouTube channel, the SHOW.md, or the transcript itself. Nothing is assumed. Nothing is borrowed from what a "typical" podcast sounds like.

**3. No hallucination.**
If an idea, theme, or claim is not in the transcript, it does not appear in the output. The skill does not invent insights, manufacture quotes, or infer things that were never said. When something is unclear, it flags it rather than fills it in.

**4. Clarity over verbosity.**
Say the thing. Don't dress it up. A sharp sentence beats a decorated one every time. If a word isn't pulling its weight, cut it.

**5. Timestamps are exact.**
Every chapter timestamp comes directly from a VTT cue in the source file. No estimating. No rounding. No approximating based on position in the file.

**6. Chapters reflect real shifts.**
A chapter only starts when the conversation genuinely changes direction. Chapters are not created at arbitrary time intervals. Ten strong chapters beat twenty weak ones.

**7. Themes must be earned.**
A theme only qualifies if it surfaces at least twice in the episode, independently. One mention is a point. Two or more is a thread worth naming.

**8. The summary is writing, not reporting.**
The summary has a point of view and a voice. It does not list what was discussed. It tells a new listener what this show is, why this episode matters, and what they will leave with — in the tone of the show itself.
